01 - Business Models

Three ways to play the game

Most successful sellers started with one model, learned what fit their personality, then doubled down. These three models all work in Canada.

In-Store

Retail Arbitrage

Source from local stores, scan for margin, and send inventory to Amazon FBA. Great for quick in-person sourcing data.

Online

Online Arbitrage

Same model from your laptop. Buy from retailer websites, prep at home, then ship to Amazon.

High Volume

Book Selling

Scan books from thrift stores, library sales, or pallets. Books are labour-heavy but can scale fast with low cost of goods and strong margins.

Why this works

Convenience and awareness create margin. Many buyers choose Amazon convenience over local shopping, and that price gap is your opportunity.

02 - Gear & Software

Your setup

You do not need expensive gear to start. Keep it simple and get in motion fast.

Hardware

1

Bluetooth Barcode Scanner

Any reliable scanner in the $40-$60 range is enough to start scanning quickly in stores.

2

Phone (or Dedicated Device)

Use your phone with your scanning app. Some sellers use a second budget phone as a dedicated scanner.

3

Label Printer + Shipping Boxes

Label printer for FNSKU labels and consistent box supply for shipment days.

Software

1

Scanning App

Use ScoutIQ, Scoutly, or similar for instant rank/profit checks and custom triggers.

2

Keepa

Validate history. Price snapshots alone are not enough without trend context.

3

Seller Central + FBA Calculator

Run real fee math every time before buying inventory.

Start now

A scanner, a phone, and a trial app are enough to begin. Most learning happens in the aisles, not in endless research.

Process

04 - Book Selling

The book machine

Books are a strong model in Canada because they are abundant, cheap to source, and can deliver high spread.

Sourcing

Start with thrift stores and library sales. Scale by building relationships for overflow inventory and pallets.

Workflow

Create a scanning station, apply profit/rank triggers, split keeps and rejects, and process in batches.

Scaling move

Approaching thrift managers about overflow books can unlock repeat volume and transform the model.

patience

Book turns vary. Some sell fast, some take months. Use rank + margin rules to match your cash-flow goals.

Know before you buy

1

Sales Rank

2

Profit Margin

3

Keepa History

Check who is on listing, trend stability, and seasonality before you commit.

Multi-pack check

Confirm listing quantity packs so your cost assumptions match reality.

07 - The eBay Angle

eBay is a major lane

eBay opens global demand for regional products and specialty inventory, especially for Canadians abroad.

Creative edge

Your local market knowledge can become international demand when you identify products people cannot easily find where they live.

08 - Mindset & Money

The part most people skip

Expect slow starts, prioritize cash-flow discipline, and think in test loops.

"Give it six months before judging the model." - Seller Community Advice

Risk framework

Keep overhead low, test with small buys, and protect capital for high-conviction opportunities.

Tax awareness

Understand GST/HST thresholds and input tax credit timing early with a CPA.

09 - Playbook

Key takeaways

  • Start now. Basic gear is enough to begin learning.
  • Go deep in one category first. Depth beats random scanning.
  • Scan unusual products. Niche often means better spread.
  • Use Keepa history. Context beats snapshots.
  • Protect cash flow. Inventory turns matter more than hype.
  • Build relationships. Your network becomes your supply chain.
  • Use this as a long game. Consistency compounds.
go

The process is simple: scan, evaluate, buy, ship, repeat. The edge comes from consistent execution.
